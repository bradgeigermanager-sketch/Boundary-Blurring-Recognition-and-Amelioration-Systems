Diagnostic Checklist for Boundary Blurring  
### Purpose  
A universal checklist you can apply to **any organization, device ecosystem, or hybrid socio‑technical system** to detect boundary blurring early.

---

## A. Ownership & Responsibility  
- Are multiple actors claiming ownership of the same process?  
- Are decisions being made at the wrong layer (strategic vs operational)?  
- Are individuals or teams acting on issues they do not control?  
- Are “shadow owners” emerging without formal authority?

**If yes:** Responsibility drift is present.

---

## B. Signal Provenance  
- Do signals lack clear source identifiers?  
- Are external device signals treated as internal organizational signals?  
- Are metrics from one domain used to evaluate another?  
- Are alerts routed to actors who cannot act on them?

**If yes:** Signal contamination is present.

---

## C. Causal Chain Integrity  
- Do root‑cause analyses frequently point to the wrong actor or device?  
- Are external events interpreted as internal failures?  
- Do device behaviors get misattributed to human decisions (or vice versa)?  
- Are shared infrastructures causing cross‑device misdiagnosis?

**If yes:** Causal chain corruption is present.

---

## D. Namespace & Identity  
- Are logs merged without device or team identifiers?  
- Are configuration files shared across domains without scoping?  
- Are automation scripts affecting multiple devices unintentionally?  
- Are organizational units reacting to device activity they do not own?

**If yes:** Namespace collision is present.

---

## E. Feedback Loop Alignment  
- Do corrective actions in one domain worsen metrics in another?  
- Do devices trigger workflows in unrelated organizational units?  
- Do organizational responses treat external noise as internal feedback?  
- Are cross‑domain loops present without explicit design?

**If yes:** Feedback loop misalignment is present.
