Boundary‑Unblurring Protocol (Repeatable Process)  
### Purpose  
A **step‑by‑step operational protocol** you can deploy in any system to restore clarity, provenance, and domain separation.

---

## Phase 1 — Identification  
1. **Collect all signals** (logs, metrics, alerts, decisions).  
2. **Tag each signal** with:  
   - Source (actor/device)  
   - Intent  
   - Target  
   - Authority to act  
3. **Map all feedback loops** across organizational and device domains.  
4. **Identify cross‑domain contamination** using the diagnostic checklist.

---

## Phase 2 — Segmentation  
5. **Rebuild namespaces**:  
   - Separate logs  
   - Separate telemetry  
   - Separate configuration files  
   - Separate automation triggers  
6. **Reassign ownership** using a Responsibility Matrix:  
   - Domain of control  
   - Domain of influence  
   - Domain of concern  
7. **Isolate layers** (policy → strategy → operations → execution).

---

## Phase 3 — Provenance Enforcement  
8. **Attach provenance metadata** to every signal:  
   - Device ID  
   - Process ID  
   - Timestamp  
   - Expected consumer  
9. **Route signals through a firewall layer**:  
   - Actionable signals → owning team  
   - Informational signals → observers  
10. **Enforce scoping** on commands and automation scripts.

---

## Phase 4 — Causal Reconstruction  
11. For each confusing event:  
   - Identify initiating actor/device  
   - Trace propagation path  
   - Identify unintended boundary crossings  
   - Rebuild the correct causal chain  
12. **Correct misattributions** in documentation and dashboards.

---

## Phase 5 — Reintegration  
13. **Rebuild cross‑domain interfaces** with explicit contracts:  
   - What devices can trigger in org units  
   - What org units can trigger in devices  
   - What signals must be ignored  
14. **Re‑align feedback loops** so each domain responds only to its own signals.  
15. **Audit the system** using the diagnostic checklist monthly.
