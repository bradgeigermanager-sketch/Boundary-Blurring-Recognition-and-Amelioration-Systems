Compliance audit checklist for boundary integrity
Use this as a periodic audit (e.g., quarterly) across org + device domains.

A. Governance & Ownership
Ownership map:

Check: Is there a current, documented map of domains of control, influence, and concern for all teams and systems?

Authority alignment:

Check: Do actors making decisions have formal authority over the affected domain?

Shadow ownership:

Check: Are there processes effectively owned by individuals/teams without explicit assignment?

B. Signal Provenance & Routing
Provenance completeness:

Check: Do all signals (alerts, metrics, commands) carry source, domain, actorId, and intent?

Routing correctness:

Check: Are actionable signals only delivered to actors with authority to act?

Cross‑domain routing:

Check: Are cross‑domain signals mediated by a defined interface (router/firewall), not direct ad‑hoc wiring?

C. Namespace & Configuration Integrity
Log segregation:

Check: Are logs and telemetry segregated by device/namespace with clear identifiers?

Config scoping:

Check: Are configuration files scoped to specific devices/services, with no unintended shared configs?

Automation boundaries:

Check: Do automation scripts declare and enforce their domain of effect?

D. Feedback Loop Design
Loop mapping:

Check: Are major feedback loops (alerts → actions → metrics) documented with owner and domain?

Cross‑domain loops:

Check: Are any loops crossing domains explicitly designed, with contracts and safeguards?

Misalignment incidents:

Check: Have there been cases where corrective actions in one domain degraded another? Are they logged and analyzed?

E. Drift‑Prevention Controls
Change review:

Check: Do integration changes undergo boundary impact assessment (who gains/loses clarity)?

Incident learning:

Check: Are boundary‑related incidents feeding back into standards and architecture updates?

Periodic re‑segmentation:

Check: Is there a scheduled review to re‑segment domains as the system evolves?
